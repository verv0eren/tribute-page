Richard Feynman

A curious character

Richard Feynman in 1984

Richard Feynman was an American theoretical physicist, a very interesting character and a great teacher. He worked on quantum physics, quantum electrodynamics, particle physics and superfluidity of liquid Helium. For his work on the development of quantum electrodynamics, he, along with other two scientists, recieved the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1965. During World War II, he assisted in the development of the atomic bomb. He was also a member of Rogers Commission, the panel which investigated the space shuttle Challenger Disaster of 1986.

If you want to know more about him. Read the following books. You will surely enjoy reading them. Note that first two books are semi-autobiographical.

  1. Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!
  2. What Do You Care What Other People Think?
  3. Tuva or Bust!
  4. Genius: The Life and Science of Richard Feynman
